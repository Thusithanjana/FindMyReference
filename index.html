How to use BibMagic

        
      

      

        
    
          
  1. 
            
    1
    
            
    
              Paste your references
              Copy one or more APA (or other style) references into the text area. Separate them with blank lines, or put each on its own line. These are often generated by LLMs, citation managers, or copy-pasted from PDFs.
            
    
          
    2. 
          
  2. 
            
    2
    
            
    
              Click “Process”
              BibMagic queries Crossref to find the real published record for each reference, then checks Unpaywall for a free legal PDF. Results appear row by row as they complete.
            
    
          
    3. 
          
  3. 
            
    3
    
            
    
              Check the flags
              If a reference looks hallucinated or incorrect, BibMagic flags exactly what is wrong — mismatched author, wrong year, title mismatch, or a low Crossref confidence score. A red row border means at least one error was found.
            
    
          
    4. 
          
  4. 
            
    4
    
            
    
              Use the links
              Scholar opens a Google Scholar search. Publisher goes to the publisher’s page. DOI.org resolves the canonical DOI. OA PDF links directly to a free full-text PDF where one exists.
            
    
          
    5. 
          
  5. 
            
    5
    
            
    
              Export BibTeX
              Click Export BibTeX to generate .bib entries for all resolved references. Copy them into your LaTeX or reference manager project.
            
    
          
    6. 
        

        

          Tip — LLM-generated references: AI tools like ChatGPT sometimes fabricate references that look plausible but don’t exist. Run them through BibMagic before citing — any mismatch between what the AI wrote and what Crossref finds will be flagged automatically.
